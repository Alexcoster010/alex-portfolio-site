About Me

With experience spanning motorsports engineering, robotic automation and manufacturing research, I thrive on projects that demand creativity, analysis and practical execution. I have led the design of Formula SAE chassis and suspension systems, built robotic cells for CNC automation, and developed advanced hardware for additive manufacturing and heat treatment. My objective is to create solutions that push performance boundaries while remaining manufacturable and reliable.

Lathe Automation Cell

Integrated an xArm6 robotic arm with a Tormach 8L CNC lathe to automate machining of Ti‑6Al‑4V tensile and fatigue samples. The cell reduces manual machine time and increases repeatability.

  • Robot motion planning and control integration
  • Pneumatic door, collet and tailstock actuation
  • Safe sequencing and handoff logic for machining operations

Designed the Sooner Racing Team vehicle chassis and suspension system to balance stiffness, weight, manufacturability and rule compliance while delivering the desired kinematic performance.

  • Full tube‑frame CAD and packaging layout
  • Suspension hardpoint optimization and roll center analysis
  • Integration with steering, drivetrain and aero subsystems

Modular Frame Fixture

Developed a modular 3D‑printed fixturing system for accurate welding and repeatable assembly of the race car chassis, improving build precision and reducing fabrication time.

  • CAD‑based layout with references to the welding table
  • ABS‑GF printed components for heat resistance and rigidity
  • Improved tack‑up accuracy and consistency during welding

Vacuum Furnace Development

Designed a vacuum heat treatment furnace for titanium parts, including the pressure vessel, vacuum system and argon backfill strategy to achieve low oxygen levels and consistent material properties.

  • ANSYS pressure analysis of the furnace chamber
  • Argon purge system and vacuum sealing strategy
  • Control and monitoring of heat treat cycles
